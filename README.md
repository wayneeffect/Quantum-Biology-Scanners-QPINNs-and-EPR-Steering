# Quantum Biology Scanner & QPINN Framework

An integrative research and simulation project exploring **quantum biology scanners**, **Quantum Physics-Informed Neural Networks (QPINNs)**, and **EPR steering** of **virtual quantum DNA** templates in higher-dimensional manifolds.

### Core Concepts
- **4D Gauge Lattice Base**: Linear/local discrete spacetime (XYZ + T) modeled as a foundational lattice.
- **6D+ Extension**: Minimal extension using **two diagonal directions** implemented as **bivector generators** (Clifford algebra) for rotational coupling.
- **Nonlinear/Nonlocal Quantum Control**: Top-down holographic superposition of universe frames through the false/quantum vacuum. Polyharmonic spline rotations (inspired by isometric camera control in games like *Final Fantasy Tactics*) enable phase-locking and probability amplitude/density-based frame selection.
- **EPR Steering & Virtual Quantum DNA**: Disease modeled as localized entropic decoherence. Health restoration via EPR steering of virtual quantum templates across reference frames.
- **Quantum Biology Scanner**: Portable, multi-mode (hyperspectral, nonlinear metasurface, quantum-enhanced) non-invasive diagnostic platform integrated with QPINN for real-time mapping and intervention.

### Implementation
- QuTiP-based quantum simulations demonstrating diagonal mixing, bivector rotations, vacuum-mediated phase-locking, and EPR correlations.
- Physics-informed neural network architecture ready for hybridization with quantum hardware.
- Relational/QBist top-down control binary protocol for selecting low-entropy (healthy) configurations.

### Goals
- Advance quantum biology and medicine through effective higher-dimensional modeling.
- Explore applications in materials science, remote sensing, biodefense, and precision health.
- Bridge classical computing, quantum simulation, and speculative-yet-grounded control techniques.

Built at the intersection of quantum information, Clifford algebra, quantum machine learning, and interdisciplinary synthesis.
